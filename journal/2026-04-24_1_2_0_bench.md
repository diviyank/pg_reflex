# 2026-04-24 — 1.2.0 Theme 1 (scoped MIN/MAX recompute) benchmark

## Scope

Theme 1 as originally planned (bounded top-K heap) was descoped mid-sprint
after a critical read: the existing `build_min_max_recompute_sql` does a full
source rescan on every retraction, and the top-K design needs ~800 lines of
new code, per-type polymorphic aggregates (or a generous SQL-level
polymorphism workaround), array-valued intermediate columns, and a
DROP+rebuild migration. Per `CLAUDE.md` priorities (correctness > simplicity
> performance), the smaller **affected-groups-scoped recompute** landed
instead.

The change (`src/trigger.rs::build_min_max_recompute_sql`): on DELETE/UPDATE
retraction, inject `AND (<raw_gb_cols>) IN (SELECT DISTINCT <norm_cols> FROM
"__reflex_affected_<view>")` **before** the `GROUP BY` in the
`orig_base_query`. An earlier attempt wrapped the query with a post-
aggregation `SELECT * FROM (<orig>) WHERE grp IN (...)`, but EXPLAIN
confirmed the planner does not push that through GROUP BY — the full source
was still aggregated.

## Driver

`/tmp/pg_reflex_1_2_0_bench.sql` — 1 M-row source, 10 000 groups, MIN/MAX
IMV in DEFERRED mode. Three trials:

- **A**: delete ~100 rows spread across 100 groups (~1 % of groups).
- **B**: delete 10 000 rows touching all 10 000 groups (100 % of groups).
- **C**: delete a single row from a single group.

Warm-up flush before Trial A.

Instance: pgrx-managed PG18 at `localhost:28818`, scratch databases per
version.

## Results

| Trial (groups affected) | 1.1.3 baseline (ms) | 1.2.0 scoped (ms) | Speedup |
|---|---|---|---|
| A — 100 / 10 000 (1 %) | 925 | **220** | **4.2×** |
| B — 10 000 / 10 000 (100 %) | 57 936 | 57 763 | 1.00× (no regression) |
| C — 1 / 10 000 (single group) | 387 | 386 | 1.00× |

Trial B confirms there is no regression when every group is affected — the
injected filter matches every row, the planner collapses the `IN` into a
semi-join over the full group set, and the work reduces to the baseline
plan. Trial C's ceiling is set by flush-framework overhead (scratch tables,
advisory locks, MERGE, target refresh) rather than the recompute itself;
at 1 group touched the recompute alone is sub-millisecond.

## EXPLAIN before/after

With the outer-WHERE wrap (first attempt):

```
HashAggregate (rows=10000) ... Group Key: t.grp
  Seq Scan on t (rows=1000000)
```

Full source still aggregated, then filtered post-aggregation — explains why
that first attempt showed no improvement (920 ms vs 925 ms baseline).

With the inject-before-GROUP-BY fix (final):

```
HashAggregate (rows=100) ... Group Key: t.grp
  Hash Semi Join
    ->  Seq Scan on t
          Filter: (grp IN (SELECT grp FROM "__reflex_affected_v"))
    ->  __reflex_affected_v scan (rows=100)
```

The filter pushes into the source scan, aggregation runs over ~100
matching rows instead of 1 M.

## Correctness

Two new `#[pg_test]`s cover the scoped path end-to-end
(`pg_test_1_2_0.rs`):

- `test_min_max_retraction_scoped_recompute_correctness` — deletes the MIN
  of one group and the MAX of another, leaves a third untouched, and
  asserts the IMV matches a fresh aggregation via `assert_imv_correct`.
- `test_min_max_retraction_empty_group_eliminated` — deletes every row of
  a group; the group must be removed from the IMV by the
  `__ivm_count <= 0` gate even though the scoped recompute never produced
  a replacement row.

All 19 MIN/MAX correctness tests pass. Full lib suite: 487 tests (up from
485 — two pg_test additions).

## Honest take

Per `CLAUDE.md` "is it worth the hassle": **yes, for narrow-retraction
workloads**. The 4.2× improvement on the 1 % case is meaningful and the
implementation cost was ~80 lines across `build_min_max_recompute_sql`,
a new `splice_before_group_by` helper, and four new unit tests. The plan's
aspirational 100× improvement required top-K, which is deferred.

The Trial C framework-overhead ceiling (~380 ms per flush for a single-
row retraction) is now the next optimization target — it dominates all
small-retraction workloads and is not addressed by scoping. Candidate
fixes (deferred, 1.3.0+): reusing scratch tables across cascade levels,
reducing plpgsql overhead inside trigger bodies, elision of advisory
locks when there is provably no contention.

## Unknowns / next steps

- **Real db_clone rerun** — the 2026-04-22 rerun captured only one
  MIN/MAX-bearing IMV (`demand_planning_characteristics_reflex`). Running
  the same trial pattern against 1.2.0 on db_clone would turn an anecdotal
  4.2× into a production-scale datapoint. Not run in this sprint session.
- **stock_chart_*_reflex views** — plan listed three "currently matview-
  only" stock_chart views that the top-K heap was supposed to unlock.
  The scoped recompute likely helps but does not unlock them: those views
  retract across many groups per flush (daily price update hitting every
  stock). Full top-K heap stays the right answer for that workload; it
  remains deferred to 1.3.0.
